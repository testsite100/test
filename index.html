Who Am I?

Welcome to ESS Academy, a soccer development academy dedicated to guiding athletes from grassroots to the professional game. We cultivate a culture that marries technical mastery with mental resilience, ensuring players not only survive but excel under elite‑level stress. Founded by Zachary Scheufele, ESS brings Barça‑inspired methodology and MLS Next‑proven results to athletes committed to a genuine, lasting transformation.

Academy Philosophy

At ESS, intelligent repetition fuels mastery. The academy empowers players with a toolbox of ball control, decisive 1v1 dominance, and tactical IQ—sharpened in sessions that fuse relentless practice with reflective learning. Players are coached to be brave, creative, and unshakable when the stakes are highest.

Academy Framework

Dynamic Ball Mastery

This cornerstone program forges complete control via creative skill combinations, tight‑area patterns, and advanced receiving & striking practices. Dominating the game requires strong technical foundations. At ESS Academy, the focus on dynamic ball mastery equips players with an explosive, refined, and versatile toolbox.

Duels

Players gain applied understanding of when and where to deploy their toolbox to outsmart and outlast opponents and maintain unpredictability during a duel.

Game‑Related Practices

Synergizing individual brilliance with collective transitions, these drills sharpen reading of game cues under real pressure. This is where individual brilliance meets collective purpose. Through possession drills, tactical scenarios, and dynamic transition play, players gain a sharper understanding of the game’s subtleties. The academy's game-related training equips students to think ahead, adapt quickly, and perform with precision under pressure.

Small‑Sided Games

Fast‑paced SSGs accelerate decision‑making speed while fine‑tuning endurance and competitive edge. With fewer players in a tighter space, each individual gets more touches on the ball and more chances to make decisions. The fast-paced, end-to-end flow of SSGs reflects the game's transitional nature—challenging attackers to create scoring opportunities and defenders to win duels and recover possession.
